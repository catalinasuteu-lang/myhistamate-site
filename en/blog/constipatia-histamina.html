Constipation and histamine: the awkward subject no one talks about

by Cătălina · MyHistamate author

It's not something people talk about much, but for me it turned out to be a key piece of the puzzle: keeping things moving in the gut.

Here's what clicked for me: when digestion is sluggish and things "sit there", histamine can be reabsorbed from the gut — in other words, it tops up the bucket from the inside. For me, the days when things weren't moving were almost always days with more symptoms.

What helped (gentle, everyday things)

  • Staying hydrated, consistently.
  • Moving — even a short walk helps things along.
  • Regular, simple meals.
  • Paying attention to the overall balance, rather than reaching for drastic "fixes".

It's not the most glamorous topic, but it was one of the things that made a real difference for me.

A practical trick: in the morning I start with a glass of water and a short walk or a few gentle stretches. Ordinary things, but consistent — and for me, consistency mattered more than any one-off "trick". When my gut is working well, the rest of the day goes better too.

Please note: this is my own personal experience, not medical advice. For persistent digestive problems, see your doctor.

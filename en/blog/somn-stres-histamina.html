Sleep, stress and histamine: the triggers that aren't food

by Cătălina · MyHistamate author

For a long time I thought it was all about what was on my plate. Then I noticed I had bad days even when I ate "perfectly" — and that the culprits were sleep and stress.

What I noticed in myself

  • A bad night fills my bucket right from the start — the next day I'm far more sensitive.
  • Stress seems to set off reactions all on its own, regardless of food.
  • Cold, intense exercise, heat can do it too.

What helped

  • I treat sleep as a priority, not a luxury.
  • During stressful stretches, I keep food simple — I know my margin is smaller.
  • Gentle movement, breathing, breaks — anything that lowers my stress also takes something out of the bucket.

The big lesson: histamine intolerance isn't only about what you eat, it's about how you live overall.

A practical trick: in the evening I do a little "wind-down" routine — dimmer lights, no screens for an hour before bed, a tea I tolerate well. It doesn't fix everything, but better sleep means an emptier bucket the next day, and that ripples into everything.

Please note: this is my own personal experience, not medical advice.
